CHDgram

Zoom using scroll, reorder using the reorder buttons or by double-clicking rows or columns, and search for a gene using the search box.
Direct and indirect PPI interactions among the protein products of mutated genes are shown as light and dark tiles, respectively. The color of a tile indicates the number of cardiac phenotypes associated with the interacting proteins - red tiles and red label-bars indicate the number of phenotypes. A gene's Samocha score is indicated by the blue triangle - the darker blue the better the score. The most promising interactions, e.g. PTPN11 and SOS1, are direct interactions among mutated proteins with good Samocha scores and that are both associated with multiple cardiac phenotypes.
The cardiac phenotypes associated with a gene knockdown in mice are shown as columns and de novo mutations are shown as rows. A gene's Samocha score is shown with a blue triangle - the darker blue the better the score. Reordering the clustergram by rank shows genes with the most phenotypes at the top. The most promising de novo mutations are those with good Samocha scores and many associated cardiac phenotypes.
